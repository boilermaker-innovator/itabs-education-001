Incident Report Procedure

Mandatory Reporting Requirements:

  • Complete Form 27B within 24 hours of incident

Required Information:

  • Date, time, and exact location of incident
  • Student(s) involved and their class
  • Description of injury or incident
  • First aid provided and by whom
  • Parent notification details

Follow-up Actions: Assistant principal will contact parents within 4 hours if injury required medical attention.

Parent Notification Protocol

Immediate Notification Required For:

  • Any injury requiring medical attention
  • Head injuries (regardless of severity)
  • Student feeling unwell and requesting to go home
  • Behavioral incidents involving suspension

Contact Priority Order:

  1. Primary emergency contact (usually parent/guardian)
  2. Secondary emergency contact if primary unavailable
  3. Authorized pickup persons if parents unavailable

Required Information:

  • Student name and class
  • Nature of incident/injury
  • First aid provided
  • Current student condition
  • Recommendation (home care, medical check, etc.)

Documentation: All parent contacts must be logged in student file with time, person contacted, and outcome.

Response Timeframes & Standards

Victorian Department Standards:

  • Urgent Issues: Same day response (safety, welfare concerns)
  • Formal Complaints: 48 hours acknowledgment, 10 days resolution
  • Academic Concerns: 5 school days for initial response
  • Policy Clarifications: 3 school days
  • Facility Issues: 24 hours acknowledgment, action as per priority

Escalation Triggers:

  • Parent dissatisfied with initial response
  • Complaint involves staff conduct
  • Legal or policy interpretation required
  • Media attention or community concern

Documentation Requirements: All communications logged, response times tracked, outcome recorded for department reporting.

Complaint Escalation Process

Escalation Pathway:

  1. Level 1: Subject teacher or relevant staff member
  2. Level 2: Year level coordinator or team leader
  3. Level 3: Assistant principal (policy interpretation)
  4. Level 4: Principal (serious concerns, staff conduct)
  5. Level 5: Regional education office (unresolved formal complaints)

When to Escalate Immediately:

  • Allegations of staff misconduct
  • Student safety concerns
  • Discrimination or harassment claims
  • Requests for policy exceptions
  • Threats of legal action or media involvement

Required Documentation: Each level must document decision rationale and actions taken before escalating.

Roll Call Procedure

Class Roll Call Process:

  1. Use class list from evacuation folder (next to classroom door)
  2. Call each student name clearly and mark attendance
  3. Account for any students not in class at time of alarm
  4. Note any students in sick bay, specialist classes, or excursions
  5. Record total present and any missing students

Missing Student Protocol:

  • Immediately report missing students to fire warden
  • Provide last known location if known
  • DO NOT return to building to search
  • Fire warden coordinates with emergency services
